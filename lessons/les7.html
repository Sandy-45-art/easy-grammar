PUNCTUATIONS

What is Punctuation?

Punctuation marks are symbols that help us understand sentences better. They show us where to pause, stop, or ask a question.

Common Punctuation Marks

Period (.)

The period is used at the end of a sentence

Example:

"The cat sleeps."

Question Mark (?)

The question mark is used at the end of a question.

Example:

"Where is the cat?"

Exclamation Mark (!)

The exclamation mark shows excitement or strong feelings.

Example:

"Wow! That’s amazing!"

Comma (,)

The comma is used to show a pause in a sentence or to separate items in a list.

Example:

""I bought apples, bananas, and oranges."

Apostrophe (')

The apostrophe is used to show that something belongs to someone or to shorten words.

Example:

"It’s my friend’s book."

Quotation Marks (" ")

Quotation marks are used to show what someone said.

Example:

She said, "I love painting."

Colon (:)

The colon introduces a list or explanation.

Example:

"You need to bring: pencils, paper, and a ruler."

Semicolon (;)

The semicolon connects two related sentences.

Example:

"I like pizza; it’s my favorite food."

Parentheses ( )

Parentheses add extra information to a sentence.

Example:

"My cat (who is very playful) loves to chase mice."

Dash (—)

The dash is used to add extra emphasis or to show a pause.

Example:

"She was late—but only by a few minutes."

Why Do We Use Punctuation?

Punctuation makes our sentences clear and easy to read. Without punctuation, sentences would run together, and it would be hard to understand what someone is saying.

Practice

Look at the sentences around you. Can you find the periods, commas, or question marks? Try writing your own sentences and use punctuation to make your writing clear.
