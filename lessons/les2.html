PRONOUNS

What are Pronouns?

Pronouns are words that take the place of a noun. Instead of saying a name or thing over and over again, you can use a pronoun.

Examples of Pronouns

I

You use "I" when you talk about yourself.

Example:

I am going to school.

You

You use "you" when you talk to someone else.

Example:

You are my friend.

He

You use "he" when you talk about a boy or a man.

Example:

He is playing with a ball.

She

You use "she" when you talk about a girl or a woman.

Example:

She painted a picture.

It

You use "it" for an object, animal, or thing.

Example:

It is a sunny day

We

You use "we" when you talk about yourself and others together.

Example:

We are having fun

They

You use "they" when you talk about more than one person or thing.

Example:

They are playing in the park.

Why Do We Use Pronouns?

Pronouns help us avoid repeating the same words. Instead of saying, "Anna is kind. Anna helps others," we can say, "Anna is kind. She helps others."

Practice

Try using pronouns in sentences you know. Instead of saying, "The dog is barking. The dog is loud," you can say, "The dog is barking. It is loud."
