ADJECTIVES

What are Adjectives?

Adjectives are words that describe nouns. They tell us more about a person, place, or thing.

Examples of Adjectives

Big

If something is large, you can say it is big.

Example:

"The elephant is big."

Small

If something is little, you can say it is small.

Example:

The mouse is small.

Happy

If someone is feeling good, you can say they are happy.

Example:

She is happy.

Sad

If someone is not feeling good, you can say they are sad.

Example:

He is sad.

Red

If something is the color red, you can describe it with this adjective.

Example:

The apple is red.

Soft

If something feels gentle or smooth, you can say it is soft.

Example:

The pillow is soft.

Why Do We Use Adjectives?

Adjectives make our sentences more interesting. They help us describe things so others can understand exactly what we mean. For example, instead of saying "the cat," you can say "the black cat" to tell us more about the cat.

Practice

Try using adjectives to describe things around you. Instead of saying "the car," you could say "the fast car" or "the blue car."
