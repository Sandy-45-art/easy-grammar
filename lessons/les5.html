Simple Sentences

What are Simple Sentences?

A simple sentence is a sentence that has just one idea. It usually has a subject (who or what the sentence is about) and a verb (what the subject is doing).

Examples of Simple Sentences:

The cat sleeps

This sentence has one idea: the cat is sleeping.

I eat apples

This sentence has one idea: I am eating apples.

She runs fast

This sentence has one idea: She is running fast.

The sun shines

This sentence has one idea: The sun is shining.

How to Make a Simple Sentence:

To make a simple sentence, you need:

  1. A subject (who or what the sentence is about).

  2. A verb (what the subject is doing).

For example

Subject: The dog

Verb: barks

Simple Sentence: The dog barks.

Practice

Try making simple sentences about things you see or do every day. For example:

"The bird sings."

"I read books."

"He plays football."
