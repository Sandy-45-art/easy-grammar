PREPOSITION

What are Prepositions?

Prepositions are words that show the relationship between a noun (a person, place, or thing) and another word in a sentence. They help us understand where something is, when something happens, or how things are related.

Examples of Prepositions

In

Shows something inside another thing.

Example:

The cat is in the box

On

Shows something is on top of something else.

Example:

The book is on the table.

Under

Shows something below another thing.

Example:

The dog is under the bed

Behind

Shows something at the back of another thing.

Example:

The car is behind the house.

Next to

Shows something beside another thing.

Example:

The chair is next to the table.

Why Do We Use Prepositions?

Prepositions help us understand the location, time, and relationship between objects in a sentence. For example:

"The apple is on the table." (Where is the apple? On the table.)

"She arrives at 5 o'clock." (When does she arrive? At 5 o'clock.)

Practice

Think about where you are sitting. Are you on a chair? Is there a book next to you? Try to use prepositions to describe the things around you.
